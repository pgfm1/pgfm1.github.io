Game List

As I play through a planned list of games, I'll add them here with a short review and impressions. Expect some good/bad according to my preferences. I will not add games until I finish them.

Lunar: Silver Star Story Complete

psx

Lunar is a simple story executed well. I think that's one of its biggest strengths - it keeps a good pace and is very focused on the main quest. There are precious few side-stories in Lunar, and honestly that works. Additionally the world changes around you (per interactions with NPCs) as the story progresses, which is a fantastic touch. The attention to detail in those interactions is superb. Paired with a very (relatively) short game, I really am happy with the execution of Lunar's story and world. If I were to play Lunar again, it would be to talk to more people at different transition points in the game, and possibly to collect items that I missed (low priority). This game also has voice acting and animated cutscenes which are fun given the era of the game. Finally, the game also keeps some (obvious, foreshadowed) secrets - this is done well and in a fun way. Nothing is particularly crazy, but the story does develop and it feels good.

Early on the combat can be challenging and interesting. Leveling up occurs frequently and has meaning. My primary complaint with this game is the combat system and its impact on the late-game: Lunar becomes boring. By the end of the game I just wanted to be done, and most of that is on the combat system, battle progression. and bosses. I was able to figure out combat early on, and it makes the combat extremely repetitive. The same set of attacks are optimal for clearing an area, and you can easily level and progress without any item expenditure after a certain point. At the same time, there is nothing "extra" you can level or progress, you don't get more abilities or strategies that make a large impact, and battles just... take time. This actually is not incredibly detrimental until the second disk, but it does kick in quite heavily in the final few areas. This is especially unfortunate because learning the combat system was fun, and early encounters required attention.

Now to discuss boss fights. Boss fights have very little variation in terms of strategy, and in some cases are pure RNG. One fight left a team member out of commission for 10+ rounds while I was AOE-spammed -- I just let my party die and tried again. The subsequent fight was trivial and one member was out for 2 rounds total. I feel like the bosses in general are very poorly-designed compared to the rest of the game. There aren't even any mechanics to break up the monotony. Vigor and sword dance are the bread and butter that will carry every boss, including and especially at the end.

Overall I'm happy that I played Lunar, but have no desire to explore the game further at this time -- due to the story being simple and short, I don't feel like I missed much of anything. I have great memories of progressing through the story, and poor memories of end-game combat. I would definitely recommend playing the game once to any fans of the genre.
